Game Analytics done Right

Using data to build compelling, playable games, maximize revenue and grow the userbase is hard

Small paragraph that shows the main issues doing game analytics

  • Data is an essential to tool identify help game designers ensure their games are as playable as possible
  • Gameplay is complicated and multi-dimensional. What constitutes a good experience is not obvious from the underlying data. It is not straightforward to use the data to identify what elements of the game or level are candidates for improving, and how to measure the impact of potential improvements.
  • Similarly, using data to optimize revenue can be challenging, esp for in-app games monetized via in-app purchases or subscriptions
  • Games are complicated and varied - the data and analysis performed for one game might look totally different to that produced for another.

Features (what)

  • Customer lifetime value
  • Player retention
  • In-app economy analytics
  • Player-to-player dynamics
  • Level and game play analytics
  • Use player behaviour to dynamically shape levels and gameplay
  • Analyse player behaviour to inform new DLC and patches

Benefits (so what?)

  • Configure and evolve the analytics to fit your game
  • Identify the right DLC and future releases for your game
  • Quickly identify parts of the game that are not performing well, and use data to fix them
  • Profitably acquire players through paid marketing channels, including algorithmic bidding
  • Optimise and adjust your gameplay in real-time using whole-population data
